Challenges in Indonesia

The Urgent Burden of Cardiovascular Disease

Indonesia faces significant challenges in early heart disease diagnosis due to its vast geography, healthcare access disparities, and limitations in specialized personnel, leading to delayed diagnoses and suboptimal patient outcomes.

üíî

High Mortality & Morbidity

  • Cardiovascular diseases are a **leading cause of death**, accounting for ~**650,000 deaths per year** (2023).
  • Stroke and Ischemic Heart Disease are major contributors to disability (DALYs).
üìà

Prevalent Risk Factors

  • **Hypertension:** 30.8% adults (2023), but low treatment adherence.
  • **Diabetes:** 11.7% adults (2023) with rising prevalence.
  • **Obesity:** Affects 1 in 3 adults (2023).
  • **Smoking:** 36.7% adult prevalence (2022), causing ~268,600 deaths/year.
ü©∫

Critical Cardiologist Shortage

  • **Fewer than 1,500 cardiologists** for over 270 million people.
  • Doctor-to-population ratio of **0.4 per 1,000**, well below WHO's 1 per 1,000.
  • **Uneven distribution:** 85% of cardiologists are in West Indonesia (65%+ in Java).
üèùÔ∏è

Geographical Access Gaps

  • Many remote islands have **less than one healthcare worker per 10,000 inhabitants**.
  • Healthcare expenditure is disproportionately higher in urban areas.
  • Urban dwellers are **1.25x more likely** to use hospital outpatient facilities than rural residents.
‚è≥

Delayed Diagnosis & Treatment

  • Over **50% of heart attack patients miss the 'golden hour'** for treatment.
  • Disparities in diagnosis and treatment adherence for chronic conditions like hypertension.
